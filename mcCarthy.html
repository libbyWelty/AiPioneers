John McCarthy

The Father of Artificial Intelligence

An interactive presentation on his life, work, and lasting legacy.

Early Life & Education

A prodigious mind with a foundation in logic and mathematics.

  • Born in Boston, MA on September 4, 1927.
  • Taught himself college-level calculus from textbooks while in high school.
  • Graduated from Caltech with a B.S. in Mathematics in 1948.
  • Earned a Ph.D. in Mathematics from Princeton University in 1951.
  • His interest in intelligent machines was sparked by the Hixon Symposium on "Cerebral Mechanisms in Behavior."

Major Contributions to AI

  • Coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" in his 1955 proposal for the Dartmouth Conference.
  • Created the Lisp programming language in 1958, which became the standard for AI research for decades.
  • Pioneered time-sharing systems, allowing multiple users to access a single computer, a precursor to the internet and cloud computing.
  • Invented garbage collection, an essential automatic memory management technique.
  • Established world-renowned AI laboratories at MIT and Stanford.

Influence on Modern AI

His foundational work continues to shape the AI landscape of today.

  • Lisp's concepts of symbolic processing and recursion are fundamental in many modern programming paradigms.
  • Time-sharing directly influenced the architecture of the internet and cloud services we use daily.
  • His research on "common sense reasoning" remains a central and ongoing challenge in creating truly intelligent AI.
  • His vision and definitions laid the entire groundwork for the field, inspiring generations of researchers.
